**Job Summary**
The Red Hat Performance Tools Engineering team is seeking a Software Engineer with a deep understanding of
Eclipse IDE and experience partnering closely with Eclipse open source communities to improve Eclipse
technology within the Red Hat product portfolio. In this role, you will facilitate and participate in collective
problem solving to help create reliable code for our rapidly advancing solutions. As a Software Engineer, you'll
play an active development role in open source communities and collaborate with developers across the globe. The

work location for this position is flexible, though candidates in North America and Europe are preferred. Home-
based, remote candidates will also be considered.

**Primary Job Responsibilities**
* Take an active role in the Eclipse, Fedora, and Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL) communities
* Work across Eclipse communities of dependencies and projects to keep Eclipse updated in Fedora and Red
Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL)
* Partner closely with the Eclipse community to forward dependency versions
* Fix bugs that specifically affect Red Hat Enterprise Linux, Fedora, and Linux users
* Develop new IDE features and enhancing existing features
* Maintain the Eclipse SDK build infrastructure upstream and within Red Hat Enterprise Linux (RHEL) and
Fedora releases
* Continue to nurture and grow the community of Eclipse packagers within Linux distributions
* Further simplify and standardize the process by which Eclipse technologies make their way into Linux
distributions
* Work upstream to improve performance of Eclipse technology in Linux environment

**Required Skills**
* 5+ years of hands-on software development and engineering experience
* Excellent technical understanding of Eclipse
* Connections and significant experience working with the Eclipse and Fedora communities
* Exposure to open source development and projects
* Prior experience working within a globally distributed development team is preferred
* Bachelor's degree in a technical field; computer science background is preferred
